THE DATA HARVEST


A big problem in education

The dropout rate of students in the world is a major issue, it is the most critical in Africa,
where education is the key to everything. A big part of the problem is the lack of ability to
follow up on the students dropping out, perhaps a sick parent, a dangerous commute to school in a turbulent region.
According to research, from the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation amongst others, shows us that monitoring students
attendance could help prevent the student dropping out of school. You could therefore decrease the dropout rate by
activating counter-measures, such as adapting the students schedule in a way that allows the student to stay in school,
perhaps basing it more on homework or other measures. Data on attendance is shown to be critical information prior to
a student dropping out.

How does the Data Harvest help solve this?

The Data Harvest is a machine readable attendance document, which will be distributed to African schools,
where the teacher will fill in attendance of students, it will then be transported to a local database,
where the document is read and registered to a database, storing all attendance data. Once you have attendance
data, the data can tell you something about which students are in danger of dropping out. From there the teacher
will put measures into place, depending on the individual student and the issues he or she faces.
